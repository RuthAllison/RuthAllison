Machine Learning - Reflections

This module was probably the one I was most concerned about/least interested in when I started the course. I couldn't see how it was applicable to my job and my lack of coding experience made the whole prospect extremely daunting. What I found was a fascinating topic that seemed very logical and that had the potential for a range of projects in my day-to-day job. Learning the coding has been hard but my team-mates in the group project have been an invaluable source of support and I've found myself becoming more confident with each task. While I struggled to write code from scratch, I reached a point with our CNN assignment where I was able to make changes to existing code because I could follow what each line was doing. This is a massive step forward for me both in terms of skill and confidence.

The teamwork in this module has been challenging due to a number of issues. We had a group of five with an eleven-hour difference in time zones. This made scheduling meetings problematic as there were only three or four hours in a day when everyone was awake and even fewer hours when everyone was available. We did manage and met weekly for our project work. I feel that the group work has made me more accepting of differences in working style. Within the group there was a marked difference between the people who wanted to get everything done in good time so it could be checked and revised; and the people who left everything until the last minute and trusted that they would produce good work under pressure. Initially, I found this very frustrating but I had to learn that I cannot be responsible for other peoples' decisions - all I can do is focus on working to the best of my ability and make sure that deadlines are met.

Overall I've really enjoyed this module and want to see how much further I can push myself to use machine learning within my projects.
